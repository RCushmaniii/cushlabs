# Prospecting Strategy Retrospective — March 28, 2026
**Source:** Chrome Extension session analysis after 3 search batches (7 prospects)

---

## What Worked

### 1. Job Board Signal Scanning (Best Repeatable Tactic)
Companies hiring "AI Automation Engineer" + "Head of Customer Experience" simultaneously = confirmed need + allocated budget + unsolved problem. This worked for Bestow and Minted and should be the **primary tactic going forward**.

**Target boards:** ashbyhq.com, lever.co, greenhouse.io
**Search pattern:** "customer experience" + "automation" in the same company's open roles
**Cadence:** Weekly, 20 minutes
**Automation:** Set up Google Alerts for `site:jobs.ashbyhq.com "AI" "customer"`

### 2. Testimonial/Client-Logo Harvesting (Best One-Time Tactic Per Vertical)
Staff Boom's website → traced their client testimonials → found Bamboo Insurance, BTIS, Mile Auto. BPOs, staffing firms, and managed services providers maintain curated lists of SMBs with operational pain. Their marketing does the targeting for you.

**How to replicate:** Find the top 3-5 service providers in a target vertical. Scan their testimonials, client logos, and case studies. Each yields 5-10 named buyer companies.

### 3. Industry Clustering (Insurance Beachhead)
5 of 7 prospects are insurance/insurtech. Not a coincidence — reflects market timing (insurtech modernization, TPA operations, policy administration). One win creates a reference case for the next. One strong case study beats seven cold emails.

## What Didn't Work

### 1. Vendor-Intent Google Searches
"Companies looking for AI consulting" and "customer service automation for small business" return vendor content (Zendesk, Salesforce, IBM articles), not buyers. These are informational/commercial queries from the vendor perspective, not buying signals.

### 2. Direct Email Discovery
Only 2 generic emails found across 7 prospects (customerservice@bambooinsurance.com, info@staffboom.com). Zero direct exec emails. **This is the single biggest gap between research and outreach.** Without solving this, the list stays theoretical.

## Higher-Leverage Plays (Beyond Cold Email)

### Play 1: Partner Channel Through Staff Boom (Highest Leverage)
Instead of selling to Staff Boom's clients directly, sell TO Staff Boom. An AI layer on top of their BPO operations makes their service better and more scalable. If they resell or refer CushLabs to their clients, you get warm intros instead of cold emails. **One partner relationship can unlock an entire client roster.**

### Play 2: Content-Led Inbound on LinkedIn
Write a specific post: "I looked at 5 insurance MGAs that are scaling fast — here's what their customer service ops look like and how AI could help." Tag Bamboo Insurance and BTIS publicly. Builds authority in the insurance vertical and gets inbound interest from others who recognize themselves.

### Play 3: Clutch.co Reverse Targeting
Look at companies that posted negative reviews of their old CS/automation vendors ("it didn't work", "we outgrew it"). These are live buyers with a bad experience who are open to alternatives. The review text tells you exactly what pain language to use.

### Play 4: Vertical Community Presence (3-6 Month Play)
Insurance communities: NAMIC, ACORD, LOMA, insurance-specific Slack/LinkedIn groups. Show up to answer questions, not pitch. Leads from this are warm and inbound.

### Play 5: LinkedIn First, Email Second
For every identified exec, a LinkedIn connection request with a short personal note is lower friction than cold email. Reference the same specific hook (TX expansion, dual hiring signal, TIME award). Connect first, email second — or let the conversation happen on LinkedIn.

### Phase 3: Outreach Cadence
- **Monday:** Send 1 new cold email (highest priority prospect)
- **Tuesday-Wednesday:** Send 1-2 more new emails
- **Thursday:** Follow up on Monday's email if no reply
- **Friday:** Review week's results, queue next week's prospects

### Phase 4: Medium-Term Plays (Start Month 2)
- LinkedIn content posts (1/week, insurance vertical focus)
- Partner outreach to Staff Boom (or similar BPOs)
- Vertical community engagement
