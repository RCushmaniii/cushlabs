## What This Service IS vs. ISN'T

### What it IS:
- Competitive monitoring: who's climbing, who's falling, what changed
- Weekly intelligence delivered to your WhatsApp (not a dashboard you'll never check)
- **Ready-to-execute action items** — not just "get more reviews" but "send this exact message to these 5 customers"
- A decision-making advantage: you know what's happening in your market before your competitors know you're watching

### What it ISN'T:
- A digital marketing agency (we don't run your ads or manage your social media)
- An SEO service (we don't optimize your website)
- A review generation platform (we don't send automated review requests)

### The Critical Middle Ground — "Intelligence + Playbooks"

Pure intelligence (just reports) churns clients fast. They feel informed but stuck — "great, my competitor has more reviews... now what?" They cancel in 2-3 months.

Full-service management doesn't scale. Managing social media, responding to reviews, creating content for 10+ clients = burnout.

**Our positioning: Intelligence + Ready-to-Execute Playbooks.**

Every weekly action item is designed to be:
- **Specific** — not "improve your reviews" but "send this message to these customers"
- **Copy-pasteable** — client can forward the WhatsApp message to their manager or paste the GBP update directly
- **Time-boxed** — takes 15-30 minutes to execute, not a project
- **Measurable** — next week's report shows if it moved the needle

## Example Weekly Action Items

These show clients that the service isn't just informational — it actively helps them improve their position.

### Review-Related Actions
- "You have 3 unanswered Google reviews from the last 2 weeks. Here are personalized response templates for each one — just copy and paste."
- "Your competitor gained 8 reviews this month. Here's a WhatsApp message you can send to your last 10 happy customers asking for a review. Just forward it."
- "You received a 2-star review mentioning wait times. Here's a response that addresses the concern professionally and invites them back."

### Google Business Profile Actions
- "Your competitor updated their GBP description with keywords 'trajes de baño tallas grandes Guadalajara.' Here's an updated description for your profile that includes similar terms matched to your brand — paste it into your GBP."
- "Businesses with 10+ photos on Google Maps get 42% more direction requests. You have 6. Upload these 4 types of photos this week: storefront, interior, product close-up, happy customer (with permission)."
- "Your GBP hours show you're closed Sundays. Your top 2 competitors are open Sundays. Consider a weekend test — if you open Sundays for 4 weeks, we'll track if it moves your ranking."

### Competitive Response Actions
- "KAANA just opened a new location in Zapopan Centro. They're getting a burst of 'new location' reviews. Push a promotion to your Zapopan customers this week to defend your position."
- "Your competitor launched a Google Offers post (10% off bikinis). Here's how to create one for your store — takes 5 minutes in your GBP dashboard."
- "Two competitors dropped below 4.0 stars this month due to complaint clusters. This is your window — push for reviews while they're weakened."

## Objection Handling

### "I can check Google Maps myself"
> "You can — and you probably do, when you remember. But you're checking your own listing, not systematically tracking 5-10 competitors every week. You're not measuring review velocity, trend lines, or seasonal patterns. And you're definitely not getting a specific action item every Monday telling you exactly what to do this week based on what changed."

### "That's too expensive for just reports"
> "It's not reports — it's intelligence plus execution playbooks. Every week you get one specific thing to do, with templates ready to use. The report takes me hours to research and analyze. The action item saves you from guessing what to focus on. And the math: if it brings you [X] customers, it's paid for — everything else is profit."

### "Can't I just hire a community manager?"
> "A community manager posts content and responds to comments. They don't monitor your competitors, they don't analyze review trends, and they don't tell you when a threat is emerging in your market. This is competitive strategy, not content management. Most clients use this alongside their community manager — we tell them what to focus on."

### "How do I know it's working?"
> "Every month you get a dashboard showing your competitive position over time. You'll see your ranking, your review count, your rating — all trending against your competitors. If you're not improving relative to your market after 3 months of executing the weekly actions, we'll have an honest conversation about whether to continue."

### "My market is different"
> "Every local market runs on the same engine: Google Maps, reviews, and visibility. Whether you're a dental clinic, a restaurant, or a boutique — customers search, compare, and choose based on what they see on Google. The specifics change. The dynamics don't."

### "I don't have time to execute the action items"
> "Each action item is designed to take 15-30 minutes. It's usually one thing: paste this response, send this message, upload these photos. You can delegate it to your store manager or receptionist — I write it so anyone on your team can execute it. If you truly can't spare 30 minutes a week to improve your competitive position, the problem isn't time — it's priorities."

## Sales Process

### Step 1: Identify Prospect
- Google Maps search for competitive verticals in target zones
- Look for: 4+ competitors visible, businesses with <100 reviews, inconsistent GBP optimization
- Bonus: businesses that respond to reviews (they care about online presence)

### Step 2: Free Competitive Snapshot
- Run a one-time competitive analysis for the prospect
- Show them: their current ranking, top 3 competitor positions, review gap, one immediate opportunity
- Deliver via WhatsApp as a voice note + screenshot
- **This is the hook.** They see their competitive position for the first time and it creates urgency.

### Step 3: Discovery Call (15-20 min)
- Walk through the snapshot findings
- Ask: "Did you know [competitor X] gained 15 reviews last month while you gained 2?"
- Explain the weekly service model
- Close with: "Would you like to try this for one month? $5,000 pesos, cancel anytime."

### Step 4: Onboarding (Day 1-3)
- Map full competitive landscape (5-10 competitors)
- Establish baseline metrics
- Set up monitoring
- Deliver first full report by Day 5

### Step 5: Retention
- Consistent Monday morning reports (never miss a week)
- Monthly dashboard review (visual proof of progress)
- Quarter 1 strategy review (adjust competitor set, refine focus)
- Upsell to multi-location if applicable

## Scaling Strategy

### Phase 1 — Manual (Now, 1-10 clients)
- Manually research competitor data weekly
- Write reports and action items personally
- Tool costs: ~$40-50 USD/client (BrightLocal, LocalFalcon)
- Time per client: 2-3 hours/week
- Capacity: 8-10 clients max at this level

### Phase 2 — Semi-Automated (3-6 months, 10-20 clients)
- Build automated data collection (Google Maps scraper, review API)
- Template-based report generation with manual insights
- Dashboard auto-generated, action items still human-written
- Time per client: 30-60 minutes/week
- Capacity: 15-20 clients

### Phase 3 — AI-Assisted (6-12 months, 20-50 clients)
- Claude API generates weekly report drafts from structured data
- Robert reviews and adds strategic insight (10-15 min per client)
- Dashboard fully automated
- Action items AI-drafted, human-approved
- Time per client: 15-20 minutes/week
- Capacity: 40-50 clients
