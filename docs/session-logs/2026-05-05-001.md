## Strategic Decisions Made This Session

### Brand Architecture
- **Single brand: CushLabs AI Services.** All products consolidate under cushlabs.ai. No more separate product domains for marketing. MarketSignal and Voice Agent become client platforms only, not separate marketed brands.
- **"I" voice stays.** Solopreneur positioning is a differentiator — no agency layers, direct accountability. Not a weakness.
- **Bilingual pricing:** USD for English pages, MXN for Spanish pages. These can be different from each other; Mexican and US markets are distinct.

### Service Lines (Final)
1. **AI Conversations** — Facebook Messenger AI (flagship) + website/WhatsApp chatbot. Same underlying tech, different deployment channels.
2. **AI Intelligence** — MarketSignal: Google Maps ranking monitoring, competitor tracking, GBP audit, one WhatsApp action per week.
3. **AI Voice** — inbound/outbound voice agents. Back-burnered for now; no rebuild until Messenger + Intelligence are systematized.
- **AI Photos: DROPPED from core.** No moat. Available as add-on only.

### Revenue Model
- Land with Messenger AI (highest daily pain for Mexican SMBs)
- Cross-sell Intelligence immediately (already delivering to first client Susy)
- Upsell Voice once the first two services are running
- Fully-stacked client target: $12,000–15,000+ MXN/month
- First client (Azúcar/Susy): staying at original pricing for trial period

### MarketSignal Platform
- marketsignal.cushlabs.ai becomes client portal ONLY
- Public marketing pages (/services, /results, /pricing, /contact) deleted; 301 redirects → cushlabs.ai
- PR #131 open in cushlabs-marketsignal (pending merge + Hetzner deploy)

## Lessons / Surprises

- **MarketSignal had better Mexican-market copy than cushlabs.ai** — the pain-first, conversational, Guadalajara-specific tone on the MarketSignal pages was superior. The best lines from that copy should be pulled into CushLabs. Don't let it disappear when the pages are retired.
- **The 4-checkmarks-for-3-problems problem was introduced in PR #90** — the original site had it too, but my hero update kept the same structure. Always audit the constraint you're trying to fix in the edit itself.
- **"Cupos limitados" is a recognizable 2015 conversion optimization cliché** — buyers (especially savvy Mexican corporate professionals) have seen it 500 times and it triggers skepticism, not urgency. Specific and believable > vague and urgent.
- **Solo consultant "I" voice converts better than fake "we"** — assessment confirms Robert's instinct. The buyer is contracting with a human who has skin in the game. Showing that human's face and name is the conversion lever, not adding a "team" layer.
